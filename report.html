How to Report GBV & TFGBV

1

Ensure safety first. Leave dangerous situations if possible and seek a trusted person.

➜
2

Document any evidence (messages, screenshots, photos, medical reports).

➜
3

Use listed hotlines or online platforms to report the case.

➜
4

Seek medical and psychological support at listed centers or clinics.

➜
5

Follow up with legal support or report to police stations with GBV desks.

Kenyan Hotlines and Reporting Platforms for GBV

  • Stop NCII

    StopNCII.org is a free tool to support victims of Non-Consensual Intimate Image (NCII) abuse. It works by generating a digital fingerprint (hash) of your intimate image(s)/video(s) on your device. This hash is then shared with participating companies to help detect and remove the images if they are shared online. The tool does not upload your actual images.

    Report via StopNCII.org

  • National Computer and Cybercrimes Coordination Committee (NC4)

    NC4 is a multi-agency entity in Kenya established to coordinate national cybersecurity matters, including the detection, prevention, response, investigation, and prosecution of computer misuse and cybercrimes.

    Report via NC4 Website

  • Healthcare Assistance Kenya (HAK) - GBV Hotline

    Provides immediate assistance and referrals for GBV survivors.

    Hotline: 1195 (24/7, toll-free)

    Email: info@hakgbv1195.org

    Visit HAK Website

  • National Police Service

    Toll-free line for reporting SGBV cases.

    Hotline: 0800730999 (toll-free)

  • Gender Violence Recovery Centre (GVRC)

    Offers medical and psychosocial support to GBV survivors.

    Hotlines: +254 719 638 006 / +254 709 667 000

    Email: info@gvrc.or.ke

    Visit GVRC Website

  • Wangu Kanja Foundation

    Provides medical, psychosocial, legal support, and financial empowerment to survivors.

    Hotline: +254722790404

  • SOLWODI (Solidarity with Women in Distress) Mombasa

    Focuses on rescuing girls from commercial sex trade, human trafficking, and forced marriages.

    Hotline: +254739422422

  • Medicins Sans Frontieres (MSF's) Lavender House Clinic (Mathare, Nairobi)

    Offers medical and psychosocial support in low-income areas.

    Contact: 020 2400330

  • Rescue Dada Centre (Ngara, Nairobi)

    Rescue, rehabilitation, and reintegration for girls from the streets.

    Contact: 0725 694 624

  • Nena Na Binti

    Provides confidential and non-judgmental SRHR support, including SGBV linkage.

    Hotlines: 0800 211 227 (Toll Free), 0775533117 (Text/WhatsApp)

    Report via Nena Na Binti

  • Uwiano Platform for Peace

    Conflict prevention strategy, includes reporting via SMS or email.

    Report via SMS to: 108

    Email: uwiano@nscpeace.go.ke

    Visit Uwiano Website

  • FIDA Kenya

    Provides legal aid and advocacy for women's rights.

    Hotline: +254 772 509 760

    Email: info@fida-kenya.org

    Visit FIDA Website

  • CREAW Kenya

    Offers legal and psychosocial support to GBV survivors.

    Toll-Free Hotline: 0800 720 186

    Email: info@creaw.org

    Visit CREW Kenya Website
